Why is the Health Sector a cybercrime target?

Another cyber criminal gold mine is the health sector. This is because in hospital files like bank accounts all of a person's personal information but unlike banks the data is not as well protected. This leads to your personal information being up for grabs and there is a lot of money to be made in the dark web when it comes to dealing with buying and selling of other persons personal information.

How Technology Improved the Crime:

  • Ransomeware - This is a cyber attack in which hackers use malware that encrypts valuable data files and then demand a ransome to release them ( FBI Wbesite, n.d). An a resent e.g of this was in 2016 when hackers hacked Hollywood Presbyterian Medical center and demanded $17,000 to restore all the hospitals applications, the doctors spent over a week without access to medical records and had to keep paper records until the ransome amount was paid( InfoSec Resources, 2016)
  • Social Engineering - In this method hackers usually target the companies that have public employee contact information and send a phishing email with an attachment, once this attachment is opened it infects the users computer and can then infect the whole computer network ( InfoSec Resources, 2016).
  • MEDJACK - This is a relatively new method being used as cyber criminals gain access by targeting medical devices with integrated applications, this can usually create a backdoor that can go undetected for a long time ( InfoSec Resources, 2016). Recent victim of this technique was UCLA Health, over 4.5 million patients personal data was exposed.
